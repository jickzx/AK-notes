# 04 - Requirements Gathering

Source: [04 - Requirements Gathering.pdf](<../Lecture Slides/04 - Requirements Gathering.pdf>)

## Core Summary

This lecture focuses on turning information gathered from users, stakeholders, briefs, and other sources into actual requirements. It distinguishes requirements from specifications and functional from non-functional requirements.

## Key Distinctions

Requirements:
- describe what users, clients, or stakeholders need;
- are problem/user/client-facing.

Specifications:
- describe how the system will satisfy the requirements;
- are more technical and guide design, implementation, and testing.

Functional requirements:
- describe what the system does.

Non-functional requirements:
- describe qualities, constraints, or limits on how the system behaves.

## Examples

Functional:
- users can submit coursework;
- staff can approve requests;
- the system sends an email confirmation.

Non-functional:
- submissions are timestamped accurately;
- personal data is access-controlled;
- pages load within an agreed time;
- the interface supports accessibility needs.

## Gathering Sources

Requirements may come from:
- project briefs;
- clients and users;
- existing systems;
- competitor/product analysis;
- market segments;
- platform rules;
- tenders;
- laws, standards, and policies.

## Exam Angles

- Do not mix requirements and specifications.
- Give concrete functional and non-functional examples.
- Explain why elicitation data must be converted into real, testable requirements.
- Treat usability, accessibility, security, reliability, performance, and maintainability as non-functional requirements.
