# 08 - Prototyping

Source: [08 - Prototyping.pdf](<../Lecture Slides/08 - Prototyping.pdf>)

## Core Summary

This lecture presents prototypes as high-level designs or partial implementations used to explore ideas, communicate with stakeholders, and develop specifications.

## Why Prototype?

Prototypes help:
- discover missing requirements;
- compare design options;
- communicate with clients/users;
- validate workflows;
- reduce uncertainty;
- test feasibility;
- refine specifications.

## Prototype Types

- Exploratory: discover requirements or compare ideas.
- Throwaway: built to learn, then discarded.
- Evolutionary: gradually developed into the final system.
- Communication prototype: helps stakeholders understand and comment.
- Technical prototype: checks feasibility or risk.

## Low- vs High-Fidelity

Low-fidelity:
- rough, cheap, fast, easy to change;
- useful early.

High-fidelity:
- realistic, detailed, often interactive;
- useful for detailed feedback;
- more costly and easier to mistake for finished software.

## Risks

- Prototype code becomes production code without quality.
- Stakeholders mistake the prototype for the finished product.
- Too much effort is spent answering a small question.
- Documentation/specification is skipped.
- The wrong stakeholders approve it.

## Exam Angles

- Explain prototype purpose before describing fidelity.
- Compare low- and high-fidelity prototypes.
- Mention benefits and risks.
- Connect prototyping to requirements, specifications, uncertainty, and stakeholder feedback.
