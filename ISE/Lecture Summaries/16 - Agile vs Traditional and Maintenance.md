# 16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance

Source: [16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf>)

## Core Summary

This lecture covers maintenance/evolution, agile vs traditional methods, Agile Manifesto values, Scrum, XP, Kanban, and how to choose methods for a scenario.

## Maintenance

Maintenance happens after delivery and includes:
- corrective changes: fault fixing;
- adaptive changes: new platform/environment/API/law;
- perfective changes: new functionality or improvements.

Maintenance is often the longest and most expensive lifecycle phase. Changes can affect requirements, specifications, architecture, code, and acceptance tests.

## Agile Values

1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation.
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation.
4. Responding to change over following a plan.

## Agile Methods

Scrum:
- backlog, sprint planning, sprint, daily scrum, Scrum Master, sprint review, retrospective, increment.

XP:
- TDD, pair programming, simple design, refactoring, CI, collective ownership, small releases, sustainable pace.

Kanban:
- board, pull-based work, changing priorities, work-in-progress limits, flow.

## Choosing a Method

Traditional fits:
- stable requirements;
- formal documentation;
- regulation;
- high risk;
- large/separate teams.

Agile fits:
- uncertainty;
- changing requirements;
- user feedback;
- incremental delivery;
- small integrated teams.

Hybrid is often strongest.
