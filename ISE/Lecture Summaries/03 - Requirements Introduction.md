## Core Summary

This lecture introduces requirements as the foundation of software engineering. Requirements define what stakeholders need and form the basis for design, implementation, testing, quality, planning, and acceptance.

Poor requirements are a major cause of project failure. Errors are much cheaper to fix early than during implementation or maintenance.

## Key Concepts

- Requirements engineering discovers, analyses, documents, and validates stakeholder needs.
- Requirements define the quality target, design basis, testing basis, and progress-tracking basis.
- Bad requirements cause rework, delays, budget overruns, and failed products.
- Missing user involvement and incomplete requirements are major failure causes.

## Stakeholders

- Primary stakeholders directly use the system.
- Secondary stakeholders are affected or involved but may not directly use it.
- Tertiary stakeholders have indirect interest, regulation, or wider influence.

Stakeholder analysis should consider priority, importance, impact, example people, and what information is needed from them.

## Personas and Use Cases

- Personas represent user groups, goals, motivations, expectations, knowledge, and differences.
- Use cases represent actors and tasks/goals performed with the system.
- These help avoid designing only for developers or managers.

## Exam Angles

- Explain why requirements are important.
- Explain why requirements defects are cheaper to fix early.
- Describe stakeholder categories.
- Use personas and use cases as elicitation/modelling tools.
