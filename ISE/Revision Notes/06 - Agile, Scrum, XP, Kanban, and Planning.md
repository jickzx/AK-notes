# Agile, Scrum, XP, Kanban, and Planning

## Agile vs Traditional

- Traditional methods include waterfall, V-model, requirements tables, detailed UML/specifications, integration testing phases, formal user testing, and separate skill teams. [L16 p47](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=47>)
- Agile methods include spiral, Scrum, iterative models, user stories, personas, rapid prototyping, TDD, CI, paired coding, frequent client interaction, and integrated skill teams. [L16 p47](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=47>)
- Traditional methods became concerning because they can be heavy, top-down, slow, document-dependent, team-separated, expensive to fix late, restrictive for developers, and hard to change during a project. [L16 p48](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=48>)
- Rapid software development interleaves specification, design, and implementation with stakeholder evaluation. [L16 p49](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=49>)
- Agile methods exist to be flexible about software engineering stages. [L16 p41](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=41>)
- Agile does not mean methods are bad; it means methods should adapt to the situation. [L16 p51](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=51>)

## Agile Values and Principles

- Agile values individuals and interactions over processes and tools. [L16 p56](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=56>)
- Agile values working software over comprehensive documentation. [L16 p56](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=56>)
- Agile values customer collaboration over contract negotiation. [L16 p56](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=56>)
- Agile values responding to change over following a plan. [L16 p56](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=56>)
- Agile principles include early and continuous delivery of valuable software. [L16 p53](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=53>)
- Agile principles welcome changing requirements, even late in development. [L16 p53](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=53>)
- Agile principles prefer frequent delivery of working software. [L16 p53](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=53>)
- Agile principles require business people and developers to work together regularly. [L16 p53](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=53>)
- Working software is the primary measure of agile progress. [L16 p54](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=54>)
- Agile should maintain a sustainable pace. [L16 p54](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=54>)

## Scrum, XP, and Kanban

- Scrum focuses on optimising by time and delivery. [L16 p64](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=64>)
- Scrum uses fixed-time sprints, often 2-4 weeks, to achieve the current plan. [L16 p71](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=71>)
- Scrum involves continuous group communication such as daily meetings. [L16 p71](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=71>)
- Scrum is managed/facilitated by a Scrum Master. [L16 p71](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=71>)
- XP focuses on team effectiveness. [L16 p64](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=64>)
- XP uses incremental planning, small releases, simple design, test-first development, refactoring, pair programming, collective ownership, continuous integration, sustainable pace, and an on-site customer. [L16 p67](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=67>) [L16 p68](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=68>)
- XP maps to agile values through customer collaboration, working software, pair programming/collective ownership, and regular releases. [L16 p69](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=69>)
- Kanban focuses on speed of feature delivery. [L16 p64](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=64>)
- Kanban uses an updating priority list and a board to manage current work. [L16 p75](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=75>) [L16 p76](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=76>)
- Kanban manages team effectiveness by limiting work in progress. [L16 p77](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=77>)
- Agile techniques can be combined, such as pair programming in Scrum or TDD in Kanban/Scrum/XP. [L16 p79](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=79>)

## Choosing Agile or Plan-Based

- A detailed specification and design before implementation points toward a traditional approach. [L16 p94](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=94>)
- Realistic incremental delivery with rapid customer feedback points toward agile. [L16 p94](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=94>)
- Small co-located teams or work that can be broken down that way point toward agile. [L16 p94](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=94>)
- Systems needing careful analysis before implementation point toward traditional methods. [L16 p95](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=95>)
- Long-lived systems that will be revised regularly may need stronger documentation. [L16 p95](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=95>)
- Remote colleagues, partner communication, process-oriented culture, and external regulation/formal standards point toward traditional methods. [L16 p96](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=96>)
- The best answer is often not "agile or plan overall" but deciding which stages need agile or formal approaches. [L16 p97](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=97>) [L16 p99](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=99>)

## Project Planning

- Project planning documents can include introduction, organisation, risk analysis, hardware/software, work breakdown, schedule, and monitoring/revision plans. [L19 p5](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=5>)
- Project planning involves defining schedule, identifying risks and constraints, defining milestones/deliverables, doing work, monitoring progress, mitigating risks, and replanning when needed. [L19 p6](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=6>)
- PERT diagrams show tasks and dependencies. [L19 p11](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=11>)
- Critical Path Method identifies schedule risk and the tasks that determine project duration. [L19 p11](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=11>)
- Gantt charts add time to tasks and dependencies. [L19 p11](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=11>)
- Staff allocation charts show who can do the tasks. [L19 p11](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=11>)
- Breaking down tasks helps estimate people, duration, and cost. [L19 p3](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=3>)

## Exam Angles

- If asked to state Agile Manifesto values, write the four values exactly and avoid confusing them with later principles. [L16 p56](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=56>)
- If asked whether agile is faster/cheaper, say not automatically; it can reduce waste through feedback but still requires rigour and can create rework. [L16 p89](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=89>) [L16 p100](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=100>)
- If asked to justify agile vs plan-based in a scenario, use the project facts: regulation, uncertainty, user access, team size, documentation need, and expected change. [L16 p94](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=94>) [L16 p95](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=95>) [L16 p96](<../Lecture Slides/16 - Agile vs Traditional and Maintenance.pdf#page=96>)
- If asked project planning maths/diagrams, focus on dependencies, Gantt chart timing, critical path, and staff allocation. [L19 p11](<../Lecture Slides/19 - Agile Planning and Project Management.pdf#page=11>)
