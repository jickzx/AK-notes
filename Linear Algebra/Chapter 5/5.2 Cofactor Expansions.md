## 5.2 Cofactor Expansions

### Minors and Cofactors

For an $n\times n$ matrix $A$, the $(i,j)$-[[Minor|minor]] $M_{ij}$ is the determinant of the matrix obtained by deleting row $i$ and column $j$.

The corresponding [[Cofactor|cofactor]] is

$$
C_{ij}=(-1)^{i+j}M_{ij}.
$$

The sign pattern is

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
+ & - & +\\
- & + & -\\
+ & - & +
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

### Cofactor Expansion

The determinant of $A$ can be computed by expanding along any row or any column.

Along row $i$:

$$
\det(A)=a_{i1}C_{i1}+a_{i2}C_{i2}+\cdots+a_{in}C_{in}.
$$

Along column $j$:

$$
\det(A)=a_{1j}C_{1j}+a_{2j}C_{2j}+\cdots+a_{nj}C_{nj}.
$$

The best row or column to choose is usually the one with the most zeros.

### Worked Example

Let

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 2 & 0\\
0 & 3 & 4\\
5 & 0 & 6
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Expand along the first row:

$$
\det(A)=1C_{11}+2C_{12}+0C_{13}.
$$

Now compute the cofactors:

$$
M_{11}=
\begin{vmatrix}
3 & 4\\
0 & 6
\end{vmatrix}
=18,
\qquad
C_{11}=18,
$$

and

$$
M_{12}=
\begin{vmatrix}
0 & 4\\
5 & 6
\end{vmatrix}
=0\cdot 6-4\cdot 5=-20,
\qquad
C_{12}=-M_{12}=20.
$$

So

$$
\det(A)=1(18)+2(20)=58.
$$

### Adjugate Formula

If $A$ is invertible, then

$$
A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det(A)}\operatorname{adj}(A),
$$

where $\operatorname{adj}(A)$ is the transpose of the cofactor matrix.

### Cramer's Rule

If $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}$ with $A$ invertible, and $A_i$ is obtained by replacing column $i$ of $A$ by $\mathbf{b}$, then

$$
x_i=\frac{\det(A_i)}{\det(A)}.
$$

This is usually more useful theoretically than computationally.
