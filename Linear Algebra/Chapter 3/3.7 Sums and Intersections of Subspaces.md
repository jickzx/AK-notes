## 3.7 Sums and Intersections of Subspaces

### Sum of Subspaces

Let $U$ and $V$ be [[Subspace|subspaces]] of $\mathbb{R}^n$.

#### Definition

Their sum is

$$
U+V=\{\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}\mid \mathbf{u}\in U,\ \mathbf{v}\in V\}.
$$

This is again a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^n$.

If $U=\operatorname{Col}(A)$ and $V=\operatorname{Col}(B)$, then

$$
U+V=\operatorname{Col}\begin{pmatrix}A & B\end{pmatrix}.
$$

So to span the sum, we can combine spanning sets for $U$ and $V$.

### Intersection of Subspaces

#### Definition

The intersection is

$$
U\cap V=\{\mathbf{x}\in\mathbb{R}^n \mid \mathbf{x}\in U \text{ and } \mathbf{x}\in V\}.
$$

This is also a subspace.

If $U=\operatorname{Nul}(A)$ and $V=\operatorname{Nul}(B)$, then

$$
U\cap V=\operatorname{Nul}\begin{pmatrix}A\\B\end{pmatrix}.
$$

So if two subspaces are described as homogeneous solution sets, their intersection is obtained by combining the equations.

### Example

Let

$$
U=\operatorname{Span}\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
0
\end{pmatrix}
\right\},
\qquad
V=\operatorname{Span}\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
1
\end{pmatrix}
\right\}.
$$

Then $U+V=\mathbb{R}^2$ because these two vectors are not collinear.

Also,

$$
U\cap V=\{\mathbf{0}\},
$$

since the two lines only meet at the origin.

### Grassmann's Formula

For subspaces $U$ and $V$ of $\mathbb{R}^n$,

$$
\dim(U)+\dim(V)=\dim(U+V)+\dim(U\cap V).
$$

This is called [[Grassmann's Formula|Grassmann's formula]].

It shows that the overlap $U\cap V$ must be subtracted when combining the dimensions of $U$ and $V$.

### Special Case

If

$$
U\cap V=\{\mathbf{0}\},
$$

then

$$
\dim(U+V)=\dim(U)+\dim(V).
$$

So dimensions simply add when the two subspaces only overlap at the zero vector.
