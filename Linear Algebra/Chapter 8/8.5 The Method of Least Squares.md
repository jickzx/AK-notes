## 8.5 The Method of Least Squares

### The Problem

If the system

$$
A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}
$$

is inconsistent, we may still want the best approximate solution.

The [[Least Squares|least-squares]] problem asks for $\hat{\mathbf{x}}$ minimizing

$$
\|A\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{b}\|.
$$

### Normal Equations

A least-squares solution satisfies

$$
A^TA\hat{\mathbf{x}}=A^T\mathbf{b}.
$$

These are called the normal equations.

### Geometric Meaning

The vector $A\hat{\mathbf{x}}$ is the [[Orthogonal Projection|orthogonal projection]] of $\mathbf{b}$ onto the [[Column Space|column space]] of $A$.

So least squares solves an inconsistent system by projecting the right-hand side onto the space of achievable outputs.

### Worked Example

Take

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
1
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\mathbf{b}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
3
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Then

$$
A^TA=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
1
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
2
\end{pmatrix},
$$

and

$$
A^T\mathbf{b}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
3
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
4
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

So the normal equation is

$$
2\hat{x}=4,
$$

giving

$$
\hat{x}=2.
$$

The projected output is

$$
A\hat{x}=
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
2
\end{pmatrix},
$$

which is the closest point in the column space to $\mathbf{b}$.

### Why This Matters

Least squares is the standard way to fit models to inconsistent data, especially in regression and approximation problems.
