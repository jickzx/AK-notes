## 8.2 Orthogonal Complements

### Definition

If $W$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^n$, its [[Orthogonal Complement|orthogonal complement]] is

$$
W^\perp=
\{\mathbf{x}\in\mathbb{R}^n:\mathbf{x}\cdot \mathbf{w}=0 \text{ for every } \mathbf{w}\in W\}.
$$

### Intuition

$W^\perp$ contains all vectors perpendicular to every vector in $W$.

### Worked Example

Let

$$
W=\operatorname{Span}
\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix}
\right\}.
$$

To find $W^\perp$, let

$$
\mathbf{x}=
\begin{pmatrix}
x\\
y
\end{pmatrix}
$$

and require

$$
\mathbf{x}\cdot
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix}
=x+2y=0.
$$

So

$$
x=-2y,
$$

and therefore

$$
W^\perp=
\operatorname{Span}
\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
-2\\
1
\end{pmatrix}
\right\}.
$$

### Key Fact

Orthogonal complements are themselves subspaces.

They are especially useful because many approximation problems are solved by requiring an error vector to lie in $W^\perp$.
