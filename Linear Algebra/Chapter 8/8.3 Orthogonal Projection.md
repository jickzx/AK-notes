## 8.3 Orthogonal Projection

### Main Idea

To project a vector onto a subspace means to find the closest vector in that subspace.

If $W=\operatorname{Span}\{\mathbf{u}\}$ with $\mathbf{u}\neq \mathbf{0}$, then the [[Orthogonal Projection|projection]] of $\mathbf{x}$ onto $W$ is

$$
\operatorname{proj}_W(\mathbf{x})=
\frac{\mathbf{x}\cdot \mathbf{u}}{\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u}.
$$

### Worked Example

Project

$$
\mathbf{x}=
\begin{pmatrix}
3\\
1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

onto the line spanned by

$$
\mathbf{u}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
1
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Compute

$$
\mathbf{x}\cdot \mathbf{u}=4,
\qquad
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{u}=2.
$$

So

$$
\operatorname{proj}_W(\mathbf{x})=
\frac{4}{2}
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
1
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
2
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The error vector is

$$
\mathbf{x}-\operatorname{proj}_W(\mathbf{x})=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
-1
\end{pmatrix},
$$

which is orthogonal to $\mathbf{u}$.

### Why This Matters

Projection decomposes a vector into:

- a part inside the subspace;
- an error part orthogonal to the subspace.

This is the geometric foundation of [[Least Squares|least squares]].
