## 8.1 Dot Products and Orthogonality

### Dot Product

For vectors $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\in\mathbb{R}^n$, the [[Dot Product|dot product]] is

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=u_1v_1+\cdots+u_nv_n.
$$

### Orthogonality

Two vectors are [[Orthogonality|orthogonal]] if

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=0.
$$

This generalizes perpendicularity.

### Norm

The length of a vector is

$$
\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{\mathbf{v}\cdot \mathbf{v}}.
$$

### Key Identity

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=\|\mathbf{u}\|\,\|\mathbf{v}\|\cos\theta.
$$

So the dot product connects algebra and geometry.

### Worked Example

Let

$$
\mathbf{u}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\mathbf{v}=
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
-1
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Then

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=1\cdot 2+2\cdot (-1)=0.
$$

So the vectors are orthogonal.

Also,

$$
\|\mathbf{u}\|=\sqrt{1^2+2^2}=\sqrt{5}.
$$

### Why This Matters

Dot products underpin:

- length and angle calculations;
- [[Orthogonal Complement|orthogonal complements]];
- [[Orthogonal Projection|orthogonal projection]];
- [[Least Squares|least squares]].
