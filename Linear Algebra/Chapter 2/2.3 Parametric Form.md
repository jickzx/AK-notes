[[Parametric Form|Parametric form]] is a way of writing the solutions of a system using one or more parameters. It becomes useful when a system has [[Free Variable|free variables]].

### Recipe: Parametric Form

The parametric form of the [[Solution Set|solution set]] of a [[Consistent System|consistent system]] of linear equations is obtained as follows:

1. Write the system as an augmented matrix.
2. Row reduce to [[RREF]].
3. Write the corresponding solved system of linear equations.
4. Move all [[Free Variable|free variables]] to the right-hand side of the equations.

### Free Variables

A [[Free Variable|`free variable`]] is a variable whose column does not contain a [[Pivot|pivot]]. A `basic variable` is a variable whose column does contain a [[Pivot|pivot]].

For example, in the matrix

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{cccc|c}
1 & 0 & {\color{orange}3} & 0 & 7 \\
0 & 1 & {\color{orange}-2} & 0 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & {\color{orange}0} & 1 & 4
\end{array}
\right)
$$

the [[Pivot|pivot]] columns are columns $1$, $2$, and $4$, so the highlighted third column has no [[Pivot|pivot]]. Therefore:

- $x_1$, $x_2$, and $x_4$ are basic variables.
- ${\color{orange}x_3}$ is a [[Free Variable|free variable]].

This means ${\color{orange}x_3}$ can be chosen freely. First, write the system in solved form, keeping ${\color{orange}x_3}$ visible inside the equations:

### Solved Form

$$
\begin{aligned}
x_1 + 3{\color{orange}x_3} &= 7 \\
x_2 - 2{\color{orange}x_3} &= 1 \\
x_4 &= 4
\end{aligned}
$$

This is not yet the [[Parametric Form|parametric form]]. It is the solved form of the system.

The solved form still describes the same geometric object, but only `implicitly`. In other words, the line or plane is contained inside the equations as a set of conditions the coordinates must satisfy.

To get the `parametric form`, assign a parameter to the free variable.

Let ${\color{orange}x_3} = {\color{orange}t}$. Then

### Parametric Form

$$
\begin{aligned}
x_1 &= 7 - 3{\color{orange}t} \\
x_2 &= 1 + 2{\color{orange}t} \\
{\color{orange}x_3} &= {\color{orange}t} \\
x_4 &= 4
\end{aligned}
$$

So the [[Solution Set|solution set]] can be written in [[Parametric Form|parametric form]] as

$$
(x_1, x_2, {\color{orange}x_3}, x_4) = (7 - 3{\color{orange}t}, 1 + 2{\color{orange}t}, {\color{orange}t}, 4).
$$

This is now `explicit`: every point in the solution set is written directly in terms of the parameter ${\color{orange}t}$.

[[Free Variable|Free variables]] are what create infinitely many solutions in a [[Consistent System|consistent system]]: once you are allowed to choose one or more variables freely, you get a whole family of solutions instead of just one.
