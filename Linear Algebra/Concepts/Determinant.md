# Determinant

## Definition

The [[Determinant|determinant]] of a square matrix $A$ is a scalar $\det(A)$ that records how the associated transformation scales signed area or volume.

## Why It Matters

- $\det(A)\neq 0$ if and only if $A$ is invertible.
- $|\det(A)|$ is the area or volume scale factor.
- The sign of $\det(A)$ tells you whether orientation is preserved or reversed.

## Example

For

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 2\\
3 & 4
\end{pmatrix},
$$

we have

$$
\det(A)=1\cdot 4-2\cdot 3=-2.
$$

So the transformation scales area by $2$ and reverses orientation.
