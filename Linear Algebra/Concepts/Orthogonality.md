# Orthogonality

## Definition

Vectors $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$ are [[Orthogonality|orthogonal]] if

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=0.
$$

## Why It Matters

Orthogonality generalizes perpendicularity and makes decompositions and projections possible.

## Example

The vectors

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix}
\quad \text{and} \quad
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
-1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

are orthogonal because

$$
1\cdot 2+2\cdot (-1)=0.
$$
