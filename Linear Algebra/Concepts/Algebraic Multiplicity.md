# Algebraic Multiplicity

## Definition

The [[Algebraic Multiplicity|algebraic multiplicity]] of an eigenvalue $\lambda$ is the number of times $\lambda$ appears as a root of the characteristic polynomial.

## Why It Matters

Algebraic multiplicity tells you how often an eigenvalue is repeated in the polynomial, but it does not by itself tell you how many linearly independent eigenvectors you get.

## Example

If the characteristic polynomial is

$$
p_A(\lambda)=(\lambda-2)^3(\lambda+1),
$$

then:

- the eigenvalue $2$ has algebraic multiplicity $3$;
- the eigenvalue $-1$ has algebraic multiplicity $1$.
