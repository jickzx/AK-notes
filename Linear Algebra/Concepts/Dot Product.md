# Dot Product

## Definition

For vectors $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\in\mathbb{R}^n$,

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=u_1v_1+\cdots+u_nv_n.
$$

## Why It Matters

The dot product measures angles, lengths, and orthogonality.

## Example

Let

$$
\mathbf{u}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\mathbf{v}=
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
-1
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Then

$$
\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}=1\cdot 2+2\cdot (-1)=0,
$$

so the vectors are orthogonal.
