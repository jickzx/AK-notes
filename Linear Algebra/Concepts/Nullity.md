# Nullity

## Definition

The [[Nullity|nullity]] of a matrix $A$ is the dimension of its [[Null Space|null space]]:

$$
\operatorname{nullity}(A)=\dim(\operatorname{Nul}(A)).
$$

Equivalently, nullity is the number of [[Free Variable|free variables]] in the equation $A\mathbf{x}=0$.

## Why It Matters

Nullity tells us how many independent input directions get sent to zero by the matrix.

If $A$ has nullity $2$, then its null space is a $2$-dimensional subspace.
