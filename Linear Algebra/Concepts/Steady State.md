# Steady State

## Definition

A [[Steady State|steady-state vector]] for a matrix $A$ is a vector $\mathbf{w}$ satisfying

$$
A\mathbf{w}=\mathbf{w}.
$$

## Why It Matters

It describes a distribution that does not change after another transition step.

In matrix language, it is an [[Eigenvector|eigenvector]] with [[Eigenvalue|eigenvalue]] $1$.

## Example

For

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
0.75 & 0.25\\
0.25 & 0.75
\end{pmatrix},
$$

the vector

$$
\mathbf{w}=
\begin{pmatrix}
1/2\\
1/2
\end{pmatrix}
$$

satisfies

$$
A\mathbf{w}=\mathbf{w}.
$$
