# Dimension

## Definition

The [[Dimension|dimension]] of a [[Subspace|subspace]] $V$ is the number of vectors in any [[Basis|basis]] of $V$.

It is written

$$
\dim(V).
$$

## Why It Matters

Dimension tells us how many independent directions a subspace has.

For example:

- a line through the origin has dimension $1$
- a plane through the origin has dimension $2$
- $\mathbb{R}^n$ has dimension $n$

If

$$
V=
\operatorname{Span}
\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
-2\\
2
\end{pmatrix},
\begin{pmatrix}
2\\
-3\\
4
\end{pmatrix}
\right\},
$$

and those two vectors are linearly independent, then $\dim(V)=2$.
