# One-to-one Transformation

## Definition

A transformation $T:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}^m$ is [[One-to-one Transformation|one-to-one]] if

$$
T(\mathbf{x})=\mathbf{b}
$$

has at most one solution for every $\mathbf{b}\in\mathbb{R}^m$.

## Why It Matters

For a matrix transformation $T(\mathbf{x})=A\mathbf{x}$, one-to-one means:

- $A\mathbf{x}=0$ has only the trivial solution
- the columns of $A$ are [[Linear Independence|linearly independent]]
- $A$ has a [[Pivot|pivot]] in every column
