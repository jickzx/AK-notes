# Geometric Multiplicity

## Definition

The [[Geometric Multiplicity|geometric multiplicity]] of an eigenvalue $\lambda$ is the dimension of its eigenspace.

Equivalently, it is the number of linearly independent eigenvectors associated with $\lambda$.

## Why It Matters

Geometric multiplicity tells you how many independent eigenvectors an eigenvalue actually provides, which is what matters for diagonalisation.

## Example

If an eigenvalue $\lambda=2$ has eigenspace

$$
\operatorname{Span}
\left\{
\begin{pmatrix}
1\\
0\\
0
\end{pmatrix},
\begin{pmatrix}
0\\
1\\
0
\end{pmatrix}
\right\},
$$

then the geometric multiplicity of $2$ is $2$.

## Key Fact

For every eigenvalue,

$$
\text{geometric multiplicity} \leq \text{algebraic multiplicity}.
$$
