
# Draft 1

On the train my phone was dying and two strangers sat next to each other. They struck up a conversation and found their analysis of the relationship between architecture and the people who use the buildings that discipline designs was consistent. They discussed emphatically in way that the discovery of conversation, agreement and understanding in the strangest of places will do to a sufficiently curious person.

 I, wishing to join these two strangers, asked them for a charger then posed them a question: which came first, the banality of architecture seeping into the people themselves, or the banality of the people themselves seeping into the buildings around them?

They were delighted that a third person had joined their conversation and the energy with which they answered each other compounded in the glow of their new subject. One of the strangers sought to reframe my thinking
