# DRAFT 2

We are all born into a house of mirrors. Look around you and you’ll find all kinds of mirrors with their own idiosyncratic distortions of reality. Some might embellish parts you hold pride in; others are less flattering.

When you are born into a house of mirrors and have lived there your entire life, how do you know what you really look like? Sure, you can feel yourself with your hands and gain an idea, but everybody agrees that the real insight comes from your reflection. How, then, does one gain a clear image of who he is if all he has to work with are distortions?

Let’s apply this to those around us. The people that we surround ourselves with are our mirrors and we ourselves reflect others in our image. Often it is described that we wear different masks around different people, but I think that we just see different distorted reflections in different sections of the house. What good would it be to describe ourselves by only one reflection, or even worse cast it all out and close your eyes altogether?

This house of mirrors is so effective at distorting our reflection on reality and ourselves that it shapes how we view the world as well, including how we reflect it onto others. In a house of mirrors, real or interpersonal, one gets the illusion that they are reflected into infinity and the space expands forever, when the influence of these mirrors is limited. Where, then, is the delineation between reality and the fictions we weave amongst ourselves between the mirrors?

Many people don’t even realise they live in a house of mirrors and take their reflection as an apparent truth when nothing could be further from it. This idea gets perpetuated by the lack of social pressure one gets when “fitting in”, where your distorted reflection is convenient or flattering enough for the house’s occupant. These people can live their entire lives unaware of its existence or pacified by its reflection, but no-one is made more painfully aware of his distortion than the social outcast gazing at mirrors that reflect a contortion of his soul back at him.
