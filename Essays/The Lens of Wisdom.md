# DRAFT 1

Slavoj Žižek criticised wisdom and idioms once, since idioms often come in contradictory pairs.

Look before you leap, fortune favours the bold.

The pen is mightier than the sword. Actions speak louder than words.

Familiarity breeds contempt, Home is where the heart is.

How are we supposed to navigate the world’s often contradictory twists and turns when everybody seems to have an opinion? Wisdom? The wise man’s job is to peer into the hearts of men and pick out the right idiom for the right moment, yet choosing the complement in the pair might have devastating effects. He suffixes his advice with, “but don’t rely on my advice too heavily, use your own judgement” and maroons each person on an island to his own devices. What really is wisdom then?

 What people call wisdom is a formless gas, colourless with an odour whose origin you can’t quite pin down. Such wisdom helps nobody save for the lucky few to receive it at the right time, so how do we give and take wisdom and navigate the world with it? How do we learn what wisdom really is?

I think that wisdom is a lens through which we view the world. I have my lens and you have yours but ultimately, we can see 20/20 in hindsight, whatever that might mean for the beholder.

When one’s lens begins to falter, he replaces it, since sometimes the beauty that was once in the eye of the beholder becomes blurry and distorted. Religions equip people with wisdom, polish and shape that lens so that it refracts light in just the right way to show a picture that others can’t quite see. That is hard to earn and even harder to return to.

When I see an event and my friend see the same, sometimes our lenses show us a different story, even when he sees things that we both know aren’t really there. A religious wisdom is bestowed from God, but how can all these lenses show the true image of reality?

Maybe reality is like a rock in the sea of human experience, and as the waves of time eat away, it is eroded into a polished idiom, washed of all its context. The generalities are woven into the exposed structure unveiled after being hidden all this time.

In the sea of human experience, smooth stones are best for skimming, but they usually don’t get very far unless they are in the hands of the skilled. Real depth comes from sinking into the water, that bedrock of experience and reality growing with age which is maturity.
